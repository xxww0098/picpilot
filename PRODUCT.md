# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PicPilot is used by ecommerce operators, designers, content makers, and small teams who need to generate, edit, compare, reuse, and publish product-oriented AI images from a self-hosted web workspace.

## Product Purpose

The product gives teams a controlled image-generation workbench: text-to-image, reference-image editing, mask editing, batch generation, local history, shared gallery workflows, and server-side API proxying that keeps upstream credentials out of the browser. Success means users can iterate on a prompt, inspect outputs, keep the useful variants, retry weak ones, and manage the resulting assets without leaving the gallery workflow.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, practical, precise. The interface should feel like a focused production tool, not a marketing showcase.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative landing-page composition, oversized hero-style typography inside the app, novelty controls, and UI treatments that make repeated production work feel slower. Avoid exposing upstream secrets or confusing users with raw provider details unless they are actively troubleshooting.

## Design Principles

- Keep generation and iteration close to the image the user is judging.
- Preserve task history and context when users retry or refine an output.
- Use familiar controls and restrained visual states for repeated workflows.
- Surface queue, error, and parameter details when they help a user decide what to do next.
- Treat local storage, credentials, and team visibility as first-class product boundaries.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to clear labels, keyboard-reachable actions, visible focus states, sufficient contrast, and reduced-motion-friendly state changes. Motion should communicate loading or replacement state only.
